History of Artificial Intelligence

From imagination to modern technology

1. Early Ideas of Artificial Intelligence

Long before computers existed, people imagined creating machines that could think like humans. In ancient myths, there were stories about artificial beings that could move and act on their own.

These ideas inspired scientists to explore whether machines could actually be intelligent. Although technology was not advanced, the idea of AI began from imagination and curiosity.

2. Birth of AI (1950s–1960s)

Artificial Intelligence officially began in the 1950s. Alan Turing asked, “Can machines think?” and introduced the Turing Test to measure machine intelligence.

In 1956, the Dartmouth Conference introduced the term “Artificial Intelligence,” marking the start of serious AI research.

3. Early AI Programs

Early AI systems followed fixed rules and could solve simple problems, but they could not learn or adapt.

ELIZA, a chatbot from the 1960s, showed that machines could communicate with humans using simple language.

4. AI Winters

AI Winters were periods when progress slowed due to lack of funding and technology limitations.

Many researchers lost interest because results did not meet expectations.

5. Rise of Machine Learning

Machine learning allowed computers to learn from data instead of fixed rules.

Deep Blue defeating Garry Kasparov in 1997 proved AI could outperform humans in complex tasks.

6. Deep Learning Revolution

Deep learning uses neural networks inspired by the human brain, allowing AI to process complex data.

AI could now recognize images, speech, and languages more accurately.

7. Modern AI

Used in smartphones and apps.

AI tools like ChatGPT support learning.

Used in healthcare and industries.
